TO-DO LIST: EISENHOWER MATRIX

What is the Eisenhower matrix?

In today's fast-paced society, the urgency to complete some tasks quickly gives the activity a false sense of importance, and people often tend to clutter their to-do lists. The Eisenhower Matrix is a novel approach to task prioritization and productivity.

Tasks are segregated into four different quadrants. The 1st quadrant holds urgent and important tasks. The 2nd quadrant holds important but not urgent tasks. The 3rd quadrant contains tasks that are not important but urgent. The 4th quadrant contains tasks that are neither urgent nor important.

How do you use the Eisenhower Matrix?

Include tasks that have a clear deadline and come with consequences for not completing them, in the first quadrant. The second quadrant emphasizes long-term objectives. The third quadrant is reserved for those tasks that have a deadline but do not bring you closer to your goals. Tasks that add to procrastination go into the fourth quadrant.

It is suggested to DO the first quadrant tasks right away, DECIDE the second quadrant tasks for completion at the appropriate time, DELEGATE the third quadrant tasks, and DELETE i.e. do not do the fourth quadrant tasks and avoid them as much as possible.
